🕷 What an Infostealer Sees

Infostealers read your browser's cookie store - typically an SQLite database file. Here's exactly what they would extract:

Loading...
Browser cookie store locations:
Chrome: %LOCALAPPDATA%\Google\Chrome\User Data\Default\Cookies
Firefox: %APPDATA%\Mozilla\Firefox\Profiles\*.default\cookies.sqlite
macOS Chrome: ~/Library/Application Support/Google/Chrome/Default/Cookies

These are SQLite databases. Infostealers like RedLine, Raccoon, and Vidar read them directly - no browser interaction needed.
Why this matters: The session cookie IS your identity to the server. It doesn't matter that you used a strong password. It doesn't matter that you entered a TOTP code. It doesn't matter that Google OAuth verified you. Once this cookie is stolen, the attacker IS you - no password, no 2FA, no OAuth required.
